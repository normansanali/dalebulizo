Career

Irish Grinstead was an American musician and R and B singer. She was a member of member of R&B girl group 702.

702, a musical group formed in the 90s, was founded by three sisters: Orish, Lemisha, and singer Amelia Childs.

The group’s name, 702, was inspired by the area code of their hometown, Las Vegas, Nevada.

Over the years, 702 went through several lineup changes, including members such as Irish Grinstead, Orish Grinstead, Amelia Cruz, Cree Le’More, and Tiffany Villarreal.

After experiencing various member configurations, the group officially disbanded in 2006.

During their prime, 702 gained popularity with hit songs like “Stello,” “Where My Girls At,” and “Pootie Tangin.”

They received multiple award nominations from prestigious events like the BET Awards and the American Music Awards.

Notably, 702 was nominated for Best Female Group at the 2001 BET Awards and Favorite Soul/R&B New Artist at the 2000 American Music Awards.

In 1997, they won the Best R&B/Soul Album of the Year at the Soul Train Lady of Soul Awards for their debut album, “No Doubt.”

In May 2021, Irish and Lemisha reunited as cast members on BET’s The Encore series.
