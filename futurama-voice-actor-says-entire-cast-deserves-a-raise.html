Futurama Voice Actor Says Entire Cast Deserves A Raise

Publish date: 2024-09-19

News that Futurama is set to rocket back onto screens next year sent fans of the cult-favorite cartoon into a frenzy, but the rival has hit a snag. Fans are threatening to boycott the series if John DiMaggio isn’t bought back to reprise his role of the foul-mouthed robot Bender. The voice actor is refusing to sign a deal until he gets paid more, and now he’s saying the entire cast deserves a bigger paycheck as well.

Last week, Hulu announced a 20-episode reboot of the show. Almost all of the original voice cast had signed on, including Katey Sagal and Billy West had agreed to return as the voices of Leela and Fry, respectively.

But the list of returning voice actors had one glaring omission. DiMaggio, who voiced the fan-favorite character Bender, has yet to ink a deal. Variety reported that DiMaggio is willing to return to the show and the producers are eager to bring him back, but negotiations have recently hit a standstill.

DiMaggio took to Twitter to address the situation on Tuesday night. He claimed that not only does he deserve more money, but that the rest of the voice actors on the show should be getting paid more too.

“I’ve been thinking about everything that’s been going on these past months, and just to be clear, I don’t think that only I deserve to be paid more. I think the entire cast does,” DiMaggio tweeted. “Negotiations are a natural part of working in show business. Everyone has a different strategy and different boundaries… Some accept offers, some hold their ground.”

DiMaggio is holding his ground and undoubtedly knows how important his character is to the series. He also has a legion of fans threatening to boycott the show if he isn’t a part of it.

This is the series second reboot. Futurama originally aired on Fox, but the network canceled the show in 2003. Comedy Central signed a deal to bring the show back in 2008 and aired original episodes until the show was axed again in 2013.

This isn’t the first time the show has had disputes with pay. After Comedy Central picked up the show, they refused to pay the voice actors the $75,000 per episode they demanded. They allegedly even threatened to hold auditions to replace the voice actors if they declined a pay cut.
