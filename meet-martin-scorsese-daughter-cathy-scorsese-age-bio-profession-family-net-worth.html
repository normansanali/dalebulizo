Cathy’s Date of Birth and Age

Cathy Scorsese was born to Martin Scorsese and Laraine Marie Brennan on December 7, 1965 in New York, United States of America. She is 57 years old.

For “The Departed,” “The Sopranos,” and other movies and TV shows, Cathy Scorsese managed the props. Martin Scorsese’s daughter has more than 30 years of experience working in the motion picture business. She has received training in on-set gun safety.

Meet Martin Scorsese daughter Cathy Scorsese

When Cathy Scorsese went with her father Marty to the Lincoln Center opening night screening of his movie “Mean Streets” for the New York Film Festival, she was only 8 years old. It was 1973, according to Cathy, and she still clearly remembers their talk after the film ended outside the cinema.

Martin, Cathy’s father, knelt down next to her on the sidewalk so that they were face to face and inquired about the movie. Martin questioned her after she had not said much, “You got any questions?” “How did they get all the blood to work like that and look so real?” Cathy found herself exclaiming. Cathy became interested in set props as a result of this occurrence.

When Cathy first had the chance to work alongside the top prop master Jim Mazzola and learn the ins and outs of the prop industry, she was in her mid-20s.

In order to make sure Cathy learnt on-set safety from the best and increased her confidence handling firearms, Jim sent her to a school on the use of theatrical weaponry taught by two well-known industry armorers.

Soon after, Cathy joined IATSE Local 52 in New York, the union for technicians working in motion picture studios, where she had to prove her familiarity with weapon safety in order to receive her union card.

Cathy has been in the industry for 30 years, and it’s safe to say that safety has been hammered into her like a reflex. This is fortunate because she has worked in props on sets with a lot of firepower, including those for “Casino,” “The Departed,” “The Sopranos,” and “Boardwalk Empire.”
