Robert J. Smith is an English singer, songwriter, record producer, and musician. Likewise, he is the lead singer of the rock band, The Cure.

Early Childhood

Robert J. Smith whose full name is Robert James Smith was born on 21st April 1959 and is currently 63 years old. His zodiac sign is Taurus. He was born in Blackpool, Lancashire, United Kingdom. He holds British citizenship. Talking about his ethnicity and religion, Smith belongs to a mixed ethnicity (English and Irish). Smith was raised as a Catholic but later became an atheist.

About his family members and siblings, he has a musical background as his father used to sing and his mother played piano. Robert is the third of the four children of James Alexander Smith and Rita Smith. Similarly, the singer has two sisters and one brother, namely Margaret, Janet, and Richard.

Education

After three, his family moved to Horley, Surrey, and attended St. Francis Primary School. He completed his middle school at Notre Dame Middle School. During his middle school, he with his schoolmates gave their first one-off performance as The Obelisk. Smith is a multi-instrumentalist who can play, drums, double bass, violin, trumpet, piano, six, and four-string bass guitars, and six and twelve-string guitars. Later, in 1972, he attended St. Wilfrids Comprehensive School. Smith completed his education by 1977.

Robert J Smith – Career and Professional Life

Robert J. Smith formed his first band when he was 14, including his brother Richard, younger sister Janet and friends. The band’s name was Crawley Goat Band. Their middle school band, The Obelisk, later became the rock band The Cure. The band members were Robert Smith on piano, Michael Dempsey on guitar, Laurence Tolhurst on percussion., Alan Hill on bass guitar, and Marc Ceccagno on lead guitar. The members called themselves malice.

Likewise, the band rehearsed Jimi Hendrix, David Bowie, and Alex Harvey songs in the local church hall. By April 1976, three members had left. The band changed its name to Easy Cure and new members Martin Creasy (vocals), Tolhurst (drums), and Pearl Thompson (guitar) joined the band. Next year in January 1977, Martin Creasy left. In 1978, it renamed the band, The Cure.

In 1979, they released their debut album Three Imaginary Boys, along with other singles like Killing an Arab, and 10:15 Saturday Night. Smith and Steve Severin of Siouxsie and the Banshees went on a UK tour to promote their second album Join Hands. In 1980, Michael Dempsey left and Matthieu Hartly (keyboard) and Simon Gallup (bass) joined the band.

Likewise, they released their third album Faith and Seventeen Seconds with their single Forest which became a hit. Robert had to shoulder the entire recording process of the band from July 1982 to February 1985. Their fourth album was Pornography which was released in 1982. In 1984, he released a solo album The top. Robert played all the instruments except the drums.

More on Career

From 2000 to 2008, he recorded new albums with The cure, which are Bloodflowers, The Cure, and 4:13 Dream. Robert is the only member who is a continuous member of The Cure. He became a lead singer as the original singer left the band. Moreover, in 2005, Robert won Ivor Novello Award for International Achievement in recognition of his songwriting and composition skills.

Later, from 2010 to 2012, he performed solo and collaborated with other artists working on solo covers like Frank Sinatra’s Witchcraft and Very Good Advice from Alice in Wonderland. In July 2013, Smith was voted the Sexiest Singer Alive by Glamour magazine. The Twilight Sad released a single featuring Robert covering There’s a Girl in the Corner in June 2015. Moreover, in 2019, he was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame as a member of The Cure.
