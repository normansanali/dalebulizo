Max Kellerman is an American boxing commentator and sports talk radio host based in Los Angeles. He has an estimated net worth of $6 million. Born in Watts, California on August 6, 1973, he grew up in New York. He attended Hunter College High School and graduated from Columbia University with a degree in History. While at Columbia, he covered professional boxing for a local public access television cable TV program called Max on Boxing. The show was quite simple yet it caught the attention of the boxing community including the young Mike Tyson.

After college, Kellerman was hired by ESPN where he covered boxing for Friday Night Fights and hosted the series Around the Horn. He was given his own show in November 2012. He also worked on a series of short-lived programs for the Fox Network, MSNBC, and Spike TV that included the highly rated I, Max and King of Vegas. He had his own program The Max Kellerman Show on ESPN Xtra after spending some time covering boxing for several radio outlets. He returned to television as part of the analysis team for HBO World Championship Boxing. He does analysis for boxing matches on HBO and serves as co-host on for the midday show in 710 ESPN.

Max Kellerman made a brief appearance in Rocky Balboa together with Lampley and Merchant. He also played as himself in the short film The Wedding Bout.
