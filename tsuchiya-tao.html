“Alice in Borderland” Actress Tsuchiya Tao Wiki | Bio

Tsuchiya Tao (born on February 3, 1995) is a 27-year-old Japanese actress, singer, dancer, model, and lyricist who has been working in the entertainment industry since 2008. Her first performance was as Mika Kurosu in “Tokyo Sonata,” a Kiyoshi Kurosawa-directed movie featuring stars like Kyôko Koizumi, Yû Koyanagi, Teruyuki Kagawa, and Kai Inowaki. But most people recognize her as Momo Ando, the character she played in a 2014 Tv series “Hanako & Anne.”

In 2018, Tao joined the cast of “Alice in Borderland (Imawa no Kuni no Arisu),” an action-packed mystery drama that aired on Netflix on December 10, 2020. The show not only received love from the viewers but also earned an IMDB rating of 7.6/10. Following the production’s success, the show’s makers came up with season two, which landed on the OTT giant on December 21, 2022.

How Old is Tsuchiya Tao?

On February 3, 1995, Tsuchiya Tao (age 27) took birth in Tokyo, Japan. She is the younger sister of the actress Honoka Tsuchiya and the elder sister of Shimba Tsuchiya. According to Japan Times, Tao received the motivation to become an actor from Tim Burton’s film “Edward Scissorhands,” but due to ankyloglossia, she kept herself away from acting.

Later she underwent surgery which improved her pronunciation and gave her the confidence to try her luck in the acting industry. At 10, she participated in and won Super Heroine Audition Miss Phoenix, earning herself the opportunity to get signed for a few TV commercials, followed by her selection as Mika in “Tokyo Sonata.”

Slowly but steadily, Tsu’s acting career grew, and by 2012 she appeared in multiple hits, including “Ryômaden,” “Suzuki sensei,” “Ouran High School Host Club,” “Sunshine,” and “The Legacy of the Sun.”

Tsuchiya Tao’s Acting Career

More and more movies got added to Tsuchiya’s career timeline with time. In 2009 she got signed for three big projects, namely “Sanpei the Fisher Boy,” “Ultraman Zero: The Revenge of Belial,” “The Legacy of the Sun” and “Ultraman Zero Gaiden: Killer the Beatstar.”

In 2015, Tao landed the lead role of Naho Takamiya in Kôjirô Hashimoto’s romantic-comedy themed “Orange.” The storyline of this big-screen project revolves around a schoolgirl who, along with her friends, learns how to be better at being friends. The acting of the young actress was top-notch, and the jury of the Japanese Academy awarded her the Newcomer of the Year award for it.

Meanwhile, she also started her music career with “Fēlicies.” People loved her voice and demanded her to keep making music, following which the talented Japanese artist released “Rules,” “Umbrella,” “Anniversary,” and “You Got Friends.”

“Orange” acted as a catalyst for her career because she witnessed immense fame and popularity after that movie. It is fascinating knowing that Tao, at the age of only 27, worked on 56 productions, including 25 shows and 31 films.
