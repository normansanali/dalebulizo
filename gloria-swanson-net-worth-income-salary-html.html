Gloria May Josephine Swanson (/swnsn/; March 27, 1899 – April 4, 1983) was an American actress, singer, and producer best remembered for her portrayal of Norma Desmond in the highly acclaimed film Sunset Boulevard (1950). She was one of the most well-known actresses and fashion icons of the silent cinema period, particularly under the supervision of Cecil B. DeMille. She appeared in hundreds of silent films and was the first woman to be nominated for an Academy Award for Best Actress. She also directed and produced films such as Sadie Thompson and The Love of Sunya. Swanson made the move to talkies in 1929 with The Trespasser. During the 1930s, when she went into theatre and television, her reputation waned due to personal difficulties and changing preferences.
