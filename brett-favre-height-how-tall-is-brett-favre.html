Brett Lorenzo Favre was born on October 10, 1969. Brett Favre was a professional quarterback for American football who spent 20 seasons in the National Football League (NFL), mostly with the Green Bay Packers. From 1992 to 2010, Favre started 321 straight games, including 297 regular-season contests, the most in league history. Brett Favre was the first quarterback in NFL history to record 70,000 yards, 10,000 throws, 6,000 completions, 500 touchdowns, 200 triumphs, and victories over all 32 teams.
In the second round of the 1991 NFL Draft, the Atlanta Falcons selected Favre, a Southern Miss football player, to be their backup quarterback for one season.

Brett Favre was acquired by the Packers in trade, taking over as their starter early in the 1992 campaign, and reviving a team that had been in decline since the late 1960s. Brett Favre guided Green Bay to 11 playoff appearances, seven division titles, four NFC Championship Games, two back-to-back Super Bowl appearances, and one championship win in Super Bowl XXXI the team’s first in three decades. Favre spent his final two seasons with the Minnesota Vikings after being traded in 2008 to the New York Jets, where Favre played for one season.
