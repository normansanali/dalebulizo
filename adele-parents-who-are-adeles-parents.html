Adele Parents: Who are Adele’s Parents? – Adele graduated from the BRIT School for Performing Arts and Technology in Croydon in May 2006, where she studied alongside Leona Lewis and Jessie J.

Despite the fact that she was more interested in going into artists and repertoire (A&R) and launching other people’s careers at the time, Adele credits the school with developing her talent.

Adele was named Songwriter of the Year at the 2012 Ivor Novello Awards in May, and “Rolling in the Deep” was named Most Performed Work of 2011.

Adele won Song of the Year at the 2012 BMI Awards in London in October, in recognition of the song being the most played on US television and radio in 2011.

Adele won the Academy Award for Best Original Song in 2013 for her work on the James Bond theme “Skyfall.” After “For Your Eyes Only” (1981), “Nobody Does It Better” (1977), “Live and Let Die” (1973), and “The Look of Love” (1974), this is the first James Bond song to win and the fifth to be nominated.

Adele was named an MBE in the Queen’s Birthday Honours list in June 2013 for services to music, and she received the award from Prince Charles at Buckingham Palace on December 19, 2013.

Woman’s Hour on BBC Radio 4 named her one of the 100 most powerful women in the UK in February 2013.

Adele’s third album, 25, was released in 2015 and quickly became the best-selling album of the year, breaking first-week sales records in a number of markets, including the United Kingdom and the United States.

25 was her second diamond-certified album in the United States, and it earned her five Grammy Awards, including her second Grammy Award for Album of the Year, as well as four Brit Awards, including her second Brit Award for British Album.

Adele became the first artist in history to win all three general categories at the Grammys on two separate occasions. Adele won more Grammys than any other female born outside the United States, with 15 awards from 18 nominations.

Adele’s seven weeks at the top of the UK Albums Chart brought her total of 31 weeks at number one in the UK with her three albums, breaking Madonna’s previous record for most weeks at number one in the UK for a female act. Within a week of its release, the lead single, “Hello,” became the first song in the United States to sell over one million digital copies.

Adele was named Songwriter of the Year for the second time by the British Academy of Songwriters, Composers, and Authors at the 2016 Ivor Novello Awards. In April 2016, she was named to the Time 100 list of the world’s most influential people for the second time.

Adele Parents: Who are Adele’s Parents?

Adele was born in Tottenham, London, to an English mother Penny Adkins, and a Welsh father Marc Evans.

Adele was two years old when Evans left, and she was raised by her mother. She began singing at the age of four and claims to have become obsessed with voices.

Adele, then nine years old, and her mother, who had found work as a furniture maker and an adult-learning activities organizer by then, moved to Brighton on England’s south coast in 1997.

She and her mother returned to London in 1999, first to Brixton, then to the neighboring district of West Norwood in south London, the subject of her first song “Hometown Glory.”
